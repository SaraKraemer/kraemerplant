Pilea Peperomiodes

Chinese Money Plant, Pancake Plant

Pilea peperomioides has bright green pancake-shaped leaves provide an adorable and welcome burst of color to any space. It is an example of a plant which has been spread amongst amateur gardeners via cuttings, without being well-known to botanists. In 1945 the species was rediscovered by a Norwegian missionary Agnar Espegren in Yunnan Province, China. Espegren took cuttings back with him to Norway and from there it spread throughout Scandinavia and beyond.

Sansevieria Trifasciata

Snake Plant, Viper's Bowstring

Sansevieria trifasciata is commonly called "snake plant" because of the shape and sharp margins of its leaves. It is also known as "viper's bowstring" because it is one of the sources for plant fibers used to make bowstrings. The NASA Clean Air Study found S. trifasciata has air purification qualities, removing 4 of the 5 main toxins!

Epipremnum Aureum

Pothos, Devil's Ivy

Epipremnum aureumis often referred to as “devil’s ivy” because it is almost impossible to kill, as it stays green even when kept in the dark. The plant is sometimes used in aquariums, placed on top of the aquarium and allowed to grow roots in the water. This is beneficial to the plant and the aquarium as it absorbs many nitrates and uses them for growth.
