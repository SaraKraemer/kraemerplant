Sansevieria Trifasciata

Snake Plant, Viper's Bowstrint

About
Sansevieria Trifasciata, also know as viper's bow string or "snake plant," is one of the most hardy, tolerant plants. They are extremely low maintenance and can also act as air purifiers to improve your home's air quality.
Pot
Snake plants do best with a free-draining soil mix because they are prone to root rot. Be sure to use a pot that won't trap water inside and promptly remove any standing water from the saucer.
Place your snake plants in indirect light, although they are tolerant of a variety of light conditions. Snake plants do well when you almost forget about them. Allow soil to dry between waterings and take extra care not to over water in winter.
Light
Snake plants produce rhyzomes that are easily divided. This is best done in the spring, as propogated plants will grow faster in the summer. You can also propagate snake plants through leaf cuttings. Just cut 2 to 3-inch pieces of a leaf and place them about 1 inch deep in soil.
Propogate
Color
When shopping for snake plants, select one that has dark green leaves; leaves that are too pale indicate that the plant may already be faltering. They can grow anywhere from 8 inches to 12 feet high.
Re-Pot
When repotting, choose a terra cotta or porous material pot and a potting mix that allows for good drainage. A little general purpose fertilizer can be used if the plants are in a pot, and that's about it!
